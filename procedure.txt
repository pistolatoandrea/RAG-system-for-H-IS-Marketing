INTRODUCTION
Our future depends on the quality of the education delivered today. The best preparation for the future is an educational approach which supports lifelong learning. At H-FARM International School, we empower students to be internationally-minded citizens and to shape their own future in a rapidly changing global community.

By instilling these habits of mind, we empower our students to become lifelong learners who continuously evolve and contribute meaningfully to society.
Here, we are not just learning languages or academic subjects; we are learning to understand each other, to bridge our differences, and to build a community that thrives on its diversity. This is why H-FARM’s mission of empowering students to transform innovation into human progress for a better society is so important.

NORD ANGLIA EDUCATION

We are proud to be the first and only Italian school group to join this global network.
Nord Anglia is not about standardisation, it is about celebrating the individuality of each school. Just like H-FARM, NAE values local culture, community, and identity, while providing the global expertise and resources that make every school stronger.

PRIMARY YEARS PROGRAMME (PYP)
The IB Primary Years Programme is an inquiry-based, transdisciplinary framework for children aged 3 to 10.
It fosters conceptual understanding and reflects the best of international educational research and best practices. Students are seen as active agents of their own learning: they are encouraged to ask questions, experiment, reflect, and take ownership of their learning journey.
The PYP nurtures the social, emotional, physical, cultural, and academic growth of every child, preparing them to engage with the challenges and opportunities of our rapidly changing world.
Through six transdisciplinary Units of Inquiry each year, students connect knowledge and skills across disciplines, developing curiosity, critical thinking, and the attributes of the IB Learner Profile.

UNITS OF INQUIRY
Students explore Units of Inquiry centred around transdisciplinary themes such as:
Who We Are


Where We Are in Place and Time


How We Express Ourselves


How the World Works


How We Organise Ourselves


Sharing the Planet


Each unit begins with a central question that sparks curiosity and invites students to explore big ideas connecting subjects like science, history, geography, art, language, maths, and technology.
Rather than memorising facts, children inquire, question, and investigate, building knowledge that is meaningful and connected to the world around them.
Teachers act as facilitators, designing authentic learning experiences that help students make connections across disciplines.
Through these inquiries, children develop:
critical thinking


collaboration


creativity


curiosity


skills that prepare them not just for school, but for life.

🌱 EARLY YEARS
In the Early Years, learning is rooted in play, exploration, and dialogue.
Learning follows the PYP curriculum framework and is enriched by inspiration from the Reggio Emilia pedagogical approach, which places the child at the center as an active, capable, and curious learner.
Through play, dialogue, and hands-on exploration, children are encouraged to:
ask questions


experiment


express themselves creatively


They learn to collaborate with peers, teachers, and families, gradually developing independence and ownership of their learning.
Each class is led by a fluent English-speaking teacher supported by an assistant, ensuring that children absorb the sounds and vocabulary of the international language during their most receptive years.
Parents are welcomed as partners in the learning journey, actively supporting their child’s growth and celebrating every step of progress.

Alex – AI for NASA Hackathon
Participating in and winning the H-FARM Hackathon as part of the NASA Space Apps Challenge was a pivotal experience for me as an aspiring software engineer.
Not only did it deepen my technical understanding of AI, but it also allowed me to strengthen essential soft skills such as collaboration, networking, and creative problem-solving. The goal of the hackathon was to develop innovative solutions to global challenges on Earth and in Space, using a collaborative and open-source approach.
I developed a machine learning model called CNN, building it completely from scratch without using any machine learning libraries. I wrote both the code and the underlying mathematics entirely on my own. Together with my team, we implemented this model on a small chip (ESP32, CiaoS3) to demonstrate its efficiency, creating a system capable of detecting the start and end of seismic events.

How We Support Students
Support strategies are designed to ensure equitable access to the curriculum and can include:
One-on-one or small group sessions, both inside and outside the classroom


Differentiated instruction and personalised learning plans


Use of assistive technology for reading, writing, and organisation


Extended time and alternative arrangements for assessments


Quiet spaces for sensory breaks or focused work


Teacher coaching to enhance classroom support


Regular communication with families and coordination with medical and external specialists


New Referrals
For students without a diagnosis, support begins when a teacher raises concerns. The process includes:
Observation and feedback collection by the Learning Support Specialist


Team review with the mentor and Head of Programme


Implementation of classroom strategies

The Primary Years Programme (PYP)
We just call it the PYP
We usually say MYP
Everyone knows it as the DP
The IB Primary Years Programme is an inquiry-based, transdisciplinary framework for children aged 3 to 10. It fosters conceptual understanding and reflects the best of international educational research and best practices. Students are seen as active agents of their own learning: they are encouraged to ask questions, experiment, reflect, and take ownership of their learning journey.
The PYP nurtures the social, emotional, physical, cultural, and academic growth of every child, preparing them to engage with the challenges and opportunities of our rapidly changing world. Through six transdisciplinary Units of Inquiry each year, students connect knowledge and skills across disciplines, developing curiosity, critical thinking, and the attributes of the IB Learner Profile.

Early Years Unit (ages 3–5)
In the Early Years, learning is rooted in play, exploration, and dialogue. Children are encouraged to Learning in the Early Years is rooted in the PYP curriculum framework and enriched by inspiration from the Reggio Emilia Children pedagogical approach, which places the child at the center as an active, capable, and curious learner. Through play, dialogue, and hands-on exploration, children are encouraged to ask questions, experiment, and express themselves creatively. They learn to collaborate with peers, teachers, and families, gradually developing independence and ownership of their learning.
Each class is led by a fluent English-speaking teacher supported by an assistant, ensuring that children absorb the sounds and vocabulary of the international language during their most receptive years. Parents are welcomed as partners in the learning journey, actively supporting their child’s growth and celebrating every step of progress.
Throughout the school year, children engage with four long-term Units of Inquiry, each extending across the entire academic year. These units explore the PYP’s transdisciplinary themes: Who We Are, How We Express Ourselves, How We Organise Ourselves, and Sharing the Planet. This structure allows children to revisit concepts over time, deepening their understanding and making meaningful connections between everyday experiences and the wider world.





Elementary (PYP1–5, ages 6-10) 
In the Elementary years, the PYP focuses on developing well-rounded learners through inquiry-based learning, critical reflection, and action. Each class has a main class teacher who is both a certified Apple Teacher and trained by the International Baccalaureate.
Alongside their classroom teacher, students are supported by subject specialists in Italian, Music, Visual Art and Physical Education. A Digital Learning Coach collaborates with teachers to integrate technology and coding across the curriculum, ensuring digital literacy and creativity are embedded in everyday learning.
Over the course of the school year, children explore six Units of Inquiry, each lasting between four to eight weeks. These units address transdisciplinary themes, while essential disciplines such as mathematics, literacy, and science are taught both within the units and as stand-alone subjects. This integrated approach ensures that students develop strong foundational skills while also making meaningful conceptual connections across areas of knowledge.

Clear learning intention and success criteria shared in student-friendly language.

The IB Diploma Programme corresponds to the last two years of High School (Grades 11-12) and completes the educational offer of H-FARM International School. The Diploma Programme is a rigorous, and challenging program designed to prepare students for a university career and for their future professional challenges and objectives. Students must choose a subject from each of the six subject areas. Each student must choose three Higher Level subjects (240 teaching hours for each subject) and three Standard Level subjects (150 teaching hours).
At the end of the course (usually in May of the second year), students must take the official IB exams for each of the subjects in their curriculum programme. The final grades (out of 7) are mostly made up of the grades achieved during the exams, as assessed by an external committee.

English Language Development

Our Aims
The IB programme fosters multilingualism, and we know that there are many advantages to speaking two or more languages: increased problem-solving ability, creativity, interpersonal skills and enhanced learning in general. Recent research highlights that skills and knowledge developed in one language can support the learning of another language. At H-Farm, we value each student’s linguistic repertoire and support them in accessing the curriculum in English while developing skills in other languages, including their home language. 

Multilingualism is a hallmark of our school: nearly 90% of students speak at least one language in addition to English. The most commonly spoken languages include Italian, Chinese, Russian, Spanish, French, German, Portuguese, Bulgarian, and Ukrainian. The school recognises language as a key to access, identity, and academic success. Through collaboration between teachers, specialists, and families, we ensure that every student’s language journey is supported throughout their learning experience at our school.

How We Work with Students and Teachers
Our EAL Centre, located in the MYP building, provides English language specialist classes to students on a daily basis. The EAL teaching team meets regularly with classroom teachers through collaboration cycles and works together to create resources for students and plan for co-teaching opportunities. 

MYP
In the MYP, H-FARM offers a tailored Booster English summer programme for students new to English. This course equips them with academic language and skills across eight IB subjects, easing their transition into school life. During the year, students participate in an EAL Specialist Programme, focused on subject-specific language development in areas such as Science, Mathematics, and Social Studies. Instruction integrates translanguaging strategies and includes targeted mini-lessons during lunchtime in our dedicated EAL Center. Students who are new to the school and at early stages of learning the language of instruction are assigned a language buddy, a peer who helps them settle in and navigate school life. The Young Interpreter Scheme creates opportunities for new students to meet with their buddy during breaks and lunch and receive help communicating any difficulties they may be experiencing, using both English and their shared home language.

DP
In the DP, we work closely with students during our Academic Writing sessions, tailored to students’ individual needs through coaching or small-group teaching. These sessions provide guidance on writing for a range of academic purposes, from analytical essays to reflective pieces, helping students develop coherent essay structures and refine their writing style. We also offer students effective strategies for vocabulary development. They build their confidence in expressing complex ideas and strengthen their oral skills as they work towards their internal assessments, with the EAL Specialist guiding their progress and giving feedback. 


Wellbeing Support 

At H-FARM International School, we believe that learning and wellbeing go hand in hand. Our Wellbeing Department is dedicated to supporting the emotional, social, and psychological growth of every student, ensuring they feel safe, heard, and equipped to navigate challenges with confidence and resilience.
Our approach is grounded in evidence-based practices and aligns with international standards for school wellbeing and child protection. We aim to foster a caring, respectful community where mental health and emotional awareness are integral parts of education.
Our Wellbeing Team
Our team includes qualified psychologists and mental health professionals with backgrounds in psychology, education, and social-emotional development. We collaborate closely with teachers, families, and external specialists to provide comprehensive and coordinated support across all school levels.
How We Support Students
The Wellbeing Department offers a tiered system of care that focuses on prevention, early intervention, and targeted support. Services include:
Individual counseling sessions to explore emotional, social, or school-related concerns in a confidential and supportive environment.


Short-term, goal-oriented interventions (behavioral coaching) aimed at promoting self-awareness, coping strategies, and emotional regulation.


Group workshops on topics such as stress management, relationships, mindfulness, digital wellbeing, and resilience


Wellbeing education initiatives integrated within the school curriculum to build empathy, communication, and self-reflection skills.


Crisis support and risk assessment when students experience emotional distress or challenging situations (i.e. panic attacks, anxiety crisis, other psychosomatic occurrences).


Collaboration with external professionals, including psychologists, psychotherapists, and psychiatrists, when specialized care is required.


Parent consultations to share insights, offer guidance, and promote consistency between home and school.


Teacher support on demand, offering advice and tools for managing classroom wellbeing and student engagement.
Boundaries and Confidentiality
Our counselors provide short-term, non-clinical support within the educational setting. The Wellbeing Department does not offer psychotherapy or formal clinical diagnosis but can refer students and families to trusted external specialists when necessary.
All counselling sessions are conducted confidentially, with information shared only on a need-to-know basis and always within safeguarding limits.

4. Academic programmes
H-FARM International School delivers the full IB Continuum on the Venezia and Vicenza campuses, and a bilingual Cambridge International programme on the Rosà campus.
4.1 Early Years Unit (EYU)
Ages 3-5, four classes named Honey Bees, Busy Bees, Reception, Rockets. Learning is rooted in play, exploration, dialogue, inspired by the Reggio Emilia pedagogical approach within the IB PYP framework. Each class led by a fluent English-speaking teacher with an assistant. Four long-term Units of Inquiry extending across the academic year.
4.2 Primary Years Programme (PYP)
Ages 3-10 (Early Years and Elementary). PYP 1-5 covers Elementary. IB inquiry-based, transdisciplinary framework. Six Units of Inquiry per year in Elementary; learning organised around six transdisciplinary themes (Who We Are, Where We Are in Place and Time, How We Express Ourselves, How the World Works, How We Organise Ourselves, Sharing the Planet). Year 5 culminates in the PYP Exhibition. Each class led by IB-trained, Apple Teacher certified main teacher, supported by specialists in Italian, Music, PE, Visual Arts plus a Digital Learning Coach.
4.3 Middle Years Programme (MYP)
Ages 11-16, Grades MYP 1 through MYP 5. Concept-based curriculum, interdisciplinary projects.
MYP 4-5 elective choice (Grades 9-10, last two MYP years):
•	40 lessons per week.
•	Core subjects (mandatory for all): English, Italian, Humanities (History or Philosophy), Sciences (Biology, Chemistry or Physics), Mathematics (Standard or Extended), PHE (Physical and Health Education).
•	Plus 6 elective subjects chosen across three thematic blocks:
•	Block A — Additional Languages: third language (Spanish, German, French, Mandarin), Computer Science, Product Design, Biology, Business Economics.
•	Block B — Maths and Arts: Maths Support, Maths Enrichment, Visual Arts, Performing Arts +, Film and Media Studies, Product Design, Business Economics.
•	Block C — STEM and Technology: Computer Science, STEM +, Digital Storytelling +, Chemistry, Business Economics.
•	Culminating experiences: MYP 4 StartUp Lab for all students; MYP 5 Personal Project.
4.4 Diploma Programme (DP)
Ages 17-18, two years (DP 1, DP 2). Final IB Continuum stage, university preparatory. Available at Venezia and Vicenza campuses. Includes regular Parents Coffee Mornings and online webinars during the year, plus 5 university fairs over the academic year.
4.5 Cambridge International programme (Rosà only)
State-recognised bilingual curriculum (Italian + Cambridge International), Cambridge Curriculum accredited since December 2021. Coverage from kindergarten through Grade 8 (international curriculum) and Grade 9 (British curriculum). Apple technology integrated school-wide.

Across all programmes, students develop on six dimensions: Learning Success, Emotional Success, Social Success, Language Success, Community Success, Preparedness for the Future. Use this framework when responding to families that ask about holistic education or non-academic development.

13. Admission funnel and standard email patterns
The admission journey typically follows these phases. The AI mostly handles Phase 1 (first contact).
Phase 1 — First contact / Inquiry (AI primary scope)
•	Trigger: lead submits a generic info form, sends an email, or starts a website chat.
•	Goal of the AI reply: confirm receipt, briefly position the school, confirm seat availability indicatively, propose a 30-minute discovery call via Calendly, link the website, optionally attach campus brochure.
•	Three sub-templates:
•	1a. Cold lead, minimal info → introduce school, ask for child age / current grade / curriculum / language preference, propose call slots.
•	1b. Lead with questions but missing key info → answer where possible, then ask clarifying questions (current school and curriculum, what matters most in the choice of new school, child interests, languages, English/Italian preference).
•	1c. Lead with rich info already provided → reassure on curriculum alignment, confirm year-group availability, propose personal tour or virtual tour, attach at-a-glance materials.
Phase 2 — Pre-tour and Post-tour
•	2a. Pre-tour: confirm date and time of visit, share campus directions, schoolbus info, list of nearby hotels, parking instructions, phone of admission referent.
•	2b. Post-tour: thank for the visit, summarise discussion (e.g. bilingual stream interest), confirm seat availability, invite next step (online application or trial day), explain that submitting the application does not commit the family.
Phase 3 — Application
•	3a. Partial online application: check-in email if family started but did not complete.
•	3b. SYSTEM autoresponder when application is completed.
•	3c. Personal email from the admissions referent confirming receipt of all documents and explaining next step (confidential report from current school via NAE link).
Phase 4 — Offer and Enrolment
•	4a. SYSTEM-generated Acceptance Offer letter (with login details to confirm enrolment from the Director General).
•	4aa. Personal confirmation from admissions referent congratulating the family and explaining next steps.
•	4b. SYSTEM auto-generated enrolment confirmation, includes Tuition Summary and timeline of payment dates.
•	4c. Personal enrolment confirmation from admissions referent: invoice notice, expected start date, orientation day, mobile app for school updates.
Phase 5 — Visa (non-EU)
•	5a. Visa Application Documents: school issues Confirmation Letter and Attestation; family must schedule embassy appointment and confirm postal address.
•	5b. Visa Arrival: notification that visa is ready at embassy, with passport and embassy confirmation email; family confirms once visa is in passport.
Phase 6-7 — Financial Aid and Scholarship
•	6. Financial Aid request: explain the Financial Aid application form must be returned with required documents; Financial Aid Committee reviews weekly, full confidentiality.
•	7. Scholarship request: H-IS does not offer scholarships for new students. Academic Merit Scholarship only for students already in Secondary school. Offer a tour or virtual discovery meeting instead.
Follow-up sequence for non-responding leads (U1 to U4)
•	U1. First follow-up — light check-in, offer further help, may include a useful article on how to choose a school.
•	U2. Second follow-up — empathy ("we understand choosing a school is challenging"), offer to connect with another parent.
•	U3. Third follow-up (Day 22) — reassure that places exist for the children's year groups but space is filling, offer a call with a teacher of the relevant year group.
•	U4. Final follow-up — close the file but reassure it can be reopened anytime; wish the children all the best.
